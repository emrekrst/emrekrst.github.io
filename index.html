About Me

I'm a BEng Electrical & Electronic Engineering student (Upper Second-Class Honours) at the University of Southampton. I specialize in Embedded Systems and Microcontroller Programming, using C/C++ to engineer efficient, real-time solutions.

Whether designing the Power Distribution System of an autonomous rover or developing a custom PCB for the Formula Student team, I focus on sustainability and efficiency. My experience includes Embedded C/C++, PCB design with KiCad, and SystemVerilog, allowing me to deliver robust systems that solve complex physical problems.

Southampton University Formula Student Team

As part of the electronics testing team, I am developing a testing PCB for the Brake System Plausibility Device (BSPD). The board replicates the vehicle's electronics, emulates sensor signals, and simulates potential failure modes to rigorously validate this safety-critical circuit.

KiCad PCB Design Electronics Testing Fault Finding

Autonomous Vehicle Power System Design

As the head of Power Systems & Electronics for this project, I am responsible of designing and validating a safe, high-reliability power rail, distributing electricity to all components including the motors, battery, and Raspberry Pi with lots of sensors. This involves conducting research, lots of electronics lab testing, and close collaboration with the mechanical and autonomy teams to ensure successful system integration.

Power Systems Safety Testing System Integration Lab Testing

Il Matto Cyber-Physical Bridge

Designed a microcontroller-based Digital-to-Analog Converter (DAC) by filtering Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) signals through a custom passive low-pass filter circuit. Implemented a closed-loop PID control system interfaced via UART serial communication with a C++ host application, utilizing real-time ADC feedback to maintain precise voltage stability against dynamic load disturbances.

Embedded C UART Serial Communications PID Control Systems Microcontroller Programming
